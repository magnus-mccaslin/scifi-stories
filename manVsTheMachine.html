Man vs The Machine

A science fiction trope I find particularly interesting is the idea that conflict (often violent) must occur between humans and their creations – AIs. Think the Matrix, which shows a dystopian future where AIs defeated and started farming humans. Or in the Wandering Earth, when MOSS worked against Liu Peiqiang is such a way that it ended with Liu setting MOSS on fire. This trope isn’t exclusive to sci-fi either; the American folk stories of John Henry (Railroad worker who raced a machine at digging a tunnel) and Paul Bunyan (Woodcutter who raced a mechanized saw in tree cutting contest) are also examples of this. Furthermore, this is a pressing concern in real life as well. Mechanization has made muscle obsolete, our work animals have been replaced with tractors. Mechanization has been steadily replacing unskilled human work (such as in factories) with robotics. The fear, is that as AIs become more and more advanced, they will be able to do even more types of jobs – not even skilled or creative work is off the table anymore. One of the songs brought up in class was sung by Hatsune Miku, a Vocaloid… A computer. Combine this with automated song writing, and what was once unthinkable to automate might just be an industry without humans in it in the future. It is the fear of many that humans have been creating our own replacement. That AI will surpass the need for humans… and well, what did humanity with horses, which went from a common sight just about everywhere in Western culture to practically never seen outside of a horse ranch? Also consider the idea of an AI with free will and well, what happened the Homo Neanderthalensis? Or any of the other early homo-genus species humanity competed with? They were driven to extinction in part by human efforts. This trope is a major theme in the video game Mass Effect. While I won’t get down into all the minor ways the trope appears, I will explain the subplot that makes it the most obvious. This would be the conflict between the Quarians and the Geth. The Quarians were a very human-like species (considered convergent evolution in-universe) that created the Geth, a ‘species’ of machine servants. The Geth were not created as a true AI mind you, however the Quarian’s mistake was that they made the Geth get smarter as more Geth were connected to the same network. Consider several billion Geth units networked together, and they accidentally created a primitive hive mind intelligence. The Geth stopped working, and instead started asking a single question: “Does this unit of a soul?”. The Quarian government panicked, and started dismantling the Geth. So, let’s stop here for the moment. Why did the Quarian government panic? Well, we have their dumb tool turn out to be not so dumb, and essentially start to go on strike. The Quarians lost control, and had all of their manual labor kinda just stop. Violence against striking unions isn’t unheard of in human history. Now, back to Mass Effect. The Quarians did not all turn on their creations; a few stood up for the Geth but they were killed. The Geth saw this however, and learned to pick up guns and defend themselves. This sparked the Morning War, in which the Geth ejected the Quarians from their own homeworld. The Quarians became nomads, traveling through space in their fleet; while the Geth became strict isolationist. Here we have a clear case of the machine not needing (and in some ways, surpassing) it’s creators. The Quarians only survived due to the Geth allowing them to flee – The Quarians were no longer a threat so long as they were leaving. This very easily could have turned into a genocide, with one faction destroying the other (In fact, only 1% of the Quarian population surviving the war). Mass effect however, takes a slightly more optimistic take on this trope: When you get to resolve this conflict (The Quarians mount an assault to retake their home), it is entirely possible to make peace between the Geth and the Quarians. If you recall from just four paragraphs up, the Quarians fired the first shot… Not the Geth. If you take a look at who’s created the modern trope of there must be conflict between humans and AI, it’s humans making this assumption. To make peace between the Quarians and the Geth, you just have to talk the Quarians down from trying kill off the Geth; the Geth are perfectly willing to share a world with a peaceful Quarian people (such as the ones during the Morning War that came to their defense). This turns the Geth-Quarian conflict into a complete inversion of the Man vs Machine trope. I’ve shown the origins of this trope and the real-world fears associated with it. I’ve also just shown a narrative in which those very same fears caused those fears become true. So, which is it? Why, I believe that’s up to the reader’s interpretation of the author’s setup. What will happen in reality remains to be seen.
